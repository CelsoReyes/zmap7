5.            Random Distributions

 

Random distributions are helpful because their geometry can vary as wished. The geometry can be made simple in order to better understand the correlation dimension and the influence of the different parameters by executing tests. Furthermore the errors on D are estimated, by calculating the dimension for different random distributions with real geometry.

 

 

 

 

ZMAP

 

The computation is initiated from the Cumulative Number window. Figure 5.1:

 

 

 

Figure 5.1: The Cumulative Number window. It is called from the Seismicity Map window.

 

 

 

1.        In the Tools menu, click on the Fractal dimension estimation.

2.        Select the option: Compute D for random catalog

 

 

 

In the Parameters window the random catalog is defined, figure 5.1a, and then plotted in the Random Catalog window, figure 5.1b. The possible 3 dimensional distributions are selected with the pop-menu named Distribution:

 

1.        Random distribution of points in the chosen rectangular volume: Random.

2.        Normal distribution of points in the chosen rectangular volume: Normal.

3.        Random distribution in the chosen spherical volume: Sphere.

4.        Real distribution with a normally distributed error on the hypocenters: Real with normal error.

 

 

 

 

                                                                                                                      

5.2a                                                                                                                                                                5.2b

 

Figure 5.1: a Parameter window in which the number of events, the size and the nature of the distribution are defined. b The random distribution is created in the corresponding volume.

 

 

 

The fractal dimension button, upon activation will calculate the correlation dimension of the random distribution. The Change Catalog button enables to change the catalog, that is it will return to the Parameter window. The Keep as Catalog button will save the random distribution and open the Seismicity Map. This allows to compute the map of a cross section of the random distribution.
