Online Supplementary Datasets for ZMAP

 

This web page lists various datasets available fro download from our anonymous ftp server. We also provided links to other data, such as online topography. If you like to share your data with other, please send mail to stefan@seismo.ifg.ethz.ch.

 

 

 


1)     Earthquake data sets and earthquake data sets with focal mechanism

 

The datasets listed below are all in ZMAP format, saved as Matlab *.mat files, and sometimes compressed. Use. Some of the datasets contain supplementary coastlines or faults. To download, click on the link and save to file, or use anonymous ftp to lahar.ethz.ch, cd zmap\data, binary and get the files you are interested in.

 

Global datasets

PDE: Worldwide dataset of PDE earthquakes. Magnitude: mb

Harvard: Harvard CMT solutions, 1977 – 2000, Magnitude:  Mw

ISC : ISC dataset for the period 1964 – 1997, Magnitude: preferred magnitude

 

USA

WUS: Western USA moment catalog, 1852-1999, compiled by the USGS earthquake hazard teams. 

SCEC_FOC: Southern California Earthquake Data Center (info), relocated by Egill Hauksson, with focal mechanism (also: Uncompressed version).

SCEC: Southern California Earthquake Data Center (info), original SCEC catalog, 1932 –2000, compressed.

NCAL: Northern California Earthquake Data Center (info), with focal mechanism (also: uncompressed versions).

Bay Area: The extended bay area, 1967-2001, from the NCAL catalog.  uncompressed.

Parkfield: 1967-2000 Parkfiled region catalog from the Northern California Earthquake Data Center (info).

PNW: Pacific Northwest earthquake catalog, compiled at the University of Washington.

Alaska: Alaska Earthquake Information Center (AEIC) network catalog, 1988-2000, uncompressed.

 

Europe

Switzerland: Swiss Seismological Service (SED) network catalog, 1975-2001, Ml, uncompressed.

Greece: National Observatory of Athens (NOA) catalog, 1964-2001.

 

Others

Sierpinski Gasket with 4000 elements: A fractal with the Haussdorf dimension of D=1.53. You need to create a cross-section to analyze the fractal dimension.

 

 

2)     Topography and Coastlines

 

gshhs_b.b Global Hierarchical Coastline Data, copy into the ./m_map/private directory.

gshhs_h.b Global Hierarchical Coastline Data, high resolution (20Mb) copy into the ./m_map/private directory.

gshhs_l.b  Global Hierarchical Coastline Data, low resolution, copy into the ./m_map/private directory.

gshhs_c.b  Global Hierarchical Coastline Data, crude resolution, copy into the ./m_map/private directory.

 

topo_6.2.img: World topography and bathymetry in 2 minute (4 km) resolution. Copy into the ./dem directory (130 Mb!).

tbase.bin  tbase.hdr and tbase.fmt  World topography and bathymetry in 5 minute resolution (Etopo5). Copy into the ./dem directory (18 Mb!).
