Introduction to ZMAP 6

 

Stefan Wiemer, ETH, 11/2001

 

It is a great pleasure for me to announce the newest version of ZMAP, version 6.0. Who would have thought, back in 1994 when the first public version of ZMAP was released, that ZMAP would still be alive in 2001? I take this, as well as the growing number of users and publications based on ZMAP, as an indication that ZMAP fills a certain gap in the toolbox of seismologists. Most of the features offered in ZMAP are neither novel nor unique, but combined into one interactive software package they turn into a powerful tool to tackle numerous aspects of quantitative seismicity analysis.

 

Of course, all of this would not have been able without the help of the people and organizations who have actively supported ZMAP over the years. Check them out by clicking here!

I have tried hard to make ZMAP as versatile, user-friendly, and accurate as possible. Nevertheless, some bugs remain and others have undoubtedly been introduced, and some features will remain cryptic to many users. If you have questions, suggestions or bug to report, feel free to send me an e-mail (stefan@seismo.ifg.ethz.ch).
