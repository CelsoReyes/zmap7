4.            The Temporal Variations of D

 

The earthquake distribution of interest is for this computation temporally divided in subsets. This is accomplished by determining a time window containing a fixed number of earthquakes that is shifted by a constant increment also defined in number of events. The correlation dimension is calculated at each increment and then plotted in function of time. The output is a plot of the correlation dimension versus time and a graph with the correlation integral curves of all subsets. The colors of the lines correspond to the correlation dimension of the subset.
